The Iowa Hawkeyes experienced a historic transformation led by superstar guard Caitlin Clark, who became the NCAA's all-time leading scorer (men's or women's) with 3,951 career points.

Under coaches Lisa Bluder and now Jan Jensen, Iowa reached back-to-back national championship games in 2023 and 2024. While falling short of the title both times, the Hawkeyes helped revolutionize women's basketball, drawing record television audiences and selling out arenas across the country.

The 2024 national championship game between Iowa and South Carolina drew 12.9 million viewers, making it the most-watched basketball game (men's or women's, college or pro) since 2019. Clark's electric style and deep three-pointers made her a cultural phenomenon.
