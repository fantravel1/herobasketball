üìñ Biography

Li Yueru is one of China's most dominant basketball players and a rising star in Asian basketball. The towering center combines size, skill, and athleticism to dominate the WCBA (Women's Chinese Basketball Association) while representing China on the international stage.

Born on February 14, 1999, in Shijiazhuang, Hebei Province, Li Yueru's exceptional height and natural talent were evident from a young age. She developed through China's elite basketball system, quickly establishing herself as one of the country's most promising young players. At 6'7" (201 cm), she possesses the size and mobility to dominate in the paint.

Li has been a key member of China's national team, competing in FIBA World Cups, Asian Games, and Olympic qualifying tournaments. Her ability to score in the post, protect the rim, and control the boards has made her essential to China's basketball program. She's part of a new generation of Chinese players working to restore the country's status as a global basketball power.

In the WCBA, Li has won championships and individual accolades while developing her game against top competition. Her performances have attracted international attention, with many viewing her as a potential WNBA prospect. As one of Asia's premier young centers, Li Yueru represents the future of Chinese basketball and the continuing evolution of the women's game in Asia.
