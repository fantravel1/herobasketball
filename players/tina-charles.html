üìñ Biography

Tina Charles is one of the greatest centers in WNBA history and the league's all-time leading rebounder. The Atlanta Dream veteran has built a Hall of Fame career on dominant post play, elite rebounding, and consistent excellence spanning over a decade.

Born in Jamaica, Queens, New York, Charles became a UConn legend, winning two NCAA championships (2009, 2010) and the 2010 Naismith Player of the Year award. The first overall pick in the 2010 WNBA Draft, she won Rookie of the Year and went on to capture the 2012 WNBA MVP award with the Connecticut Sun, averaging a career-high 23.7 points and 9.6 rebounds per game.

Throughout her career with Connecticut, New York, Washington, Phoenix, Seattle, and now Atlanta, Charles has been a model of consistency and durability. She's an eight-time All-Star, four-time All-WNBA First Team selection, and surpassed Lisa Leslie to become the WNBA's all-time leading rebounder. Beyond basketball, she's a dedicated philanthropist through her Hopey's Heart Foundation, honoring her late aunt and helping children with heart conditions.
