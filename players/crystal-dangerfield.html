üìñ Biography

Crystal Dangerfield burst onto the WNBA scene in 2020, winning Rookie of the Year honors during a challenging bubble season. Known for her explosive scoring ability, quick first step, and fearless approach to the game, Dangerfield has established herself as a dynamic guard in the league.

Born on May 11, 1998, in Murfreesboro, Tennessee, Dangerfield played her college basketball at UConn, where she was part of the storied Huskies program. As a freshman, she helped UConn win the 2016 NCAA Championship, adding to the program's legendary history. Her leadership and scoring ability continued to develop throughout her college career.

Selected 16th overall by the Minnesota Lynx in the 2020 WNBA Draft, Dangerfield immediately made an impact, averaging 16.2 points and 3.6 assists per game to earn Rookie of the Year honors. Her ability to score at all three levels and create her own shot made her a vital piece of the Lynx's backcourt.

After stints with the Lynx and Atlanta Dream, Dangerfield joined the Indiana Fever in 2024, where she provides veteran scoring punch and playoff experience to complement the team's young stars. Her quickness, scoring ability, and championship pedigree make her a valuable asset for the Fever.
