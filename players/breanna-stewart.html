üìñ Biography

Breanna Stewart is widely considered one of the greatest players in women's basketball history. The 6'4" forward combines elite scoring, rebounding, and defensive versatility that makes her nearly impossible to stop.

Born on August 27, 1994, in Syracuse, New York, Stewart dominated at UConn, winning an unprecedented four consecutive NCAA championships (2013-2016) under Geno Auriemma and earning Final Four Most Outstanding Player honors all four years.

After being selected first overall by the Seattle Storm in 2016, Stewart won two WNBA championships (2018, 2020) and two MVP awards. In 2023, she joined the New York Liberty as a free agent and led them to the 2023 WNBA Finals, winning the 2023 Commissioner's Cup.
