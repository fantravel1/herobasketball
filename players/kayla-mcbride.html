üìñ Biography

Kayla McBride is an Olympic gold medalist and one of the most consistent scorers in the WNBA. As a key member of the Minnesota Lynx, McBride has established herself as an elite shooting guard who can score from anywhere on the court and is known for her clutch performances and professionalism.

Born on June 25, 1992, in Erie, Pennsylvania, McBride was a standout at the University of Notre Dame where she won the 2013 NCAA Championship and was a two-time Wade Trophy finalist. Her scoring ability and versatility made her a top prospect in the 2014 WNBA Draft.

Selected 3rd overall by the San Antonio Stars, McBride earned All-Rookie Team honors before joining the Las Vegas Aces and eventually the Minnesota Lynx in 2022. In 2024, she was selected to represent Team USA at the Paris Olympics, helping the team win gold while averaging 14.6 points per game for the Lynx and solidifying her status as one of the league's premier shooting guards.
