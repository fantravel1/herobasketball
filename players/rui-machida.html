üìñ Biography

Rui Machida is Japan's greatest basketball export and a symbol of the rise of Asian women's basketball on the global stage. The diminutive point guard has captivated audiences with her exceptional court vision, precise passing, and intelligent playmaking.

Born on May 21, 1993, in Tokyo, Japan, Machida developed her game in Japan's domestic leagues before becoming a national hero during the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. Her brilliant performances helped Japan win a historic Olympic silver medal, their best-ever finish, with Machida orchestrating the offense and showcasing world-class playmaking.

At just 5'4" (162 cm), Machida has proven that elite basketball IQ and skill can overcome physical limitations. Her ability to run an offense, deliver pinpoint passes, and make clutch plays has made her one of Asia's most celebrated athletes. In 2024, she signed with the Indiana Fever, becoming one of the few Japanese players to compete in the WNBA.

A multiple-time Asian Games MVP and FIBA Asia Cup champion, Machida has dominated continental competition while elevating Japanese basketball to unprecedented heights. Her success has inspired a new generation of Asian players and proven that the gap between Asian and Western basketball continues to close. She remains the face of Japanese basketball and a global ambassador for the sport.
