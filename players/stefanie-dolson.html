üìñ Biography

Stefanie Dolson is a versatile center who has become one of women's basketball's most accomplished and beloved players. The Washington Mystics veteran brings championship pedigree, Olympic glory, and a vibrant personality that has made her a fan favorite throughout her career.

Born in Port Jervis, New York, Dolson became a UConn legend, winning two NCAA championships (2013, 2014) under Geno Auriemma. Her ability to stretch the floor with three-point shooting while maintaining traditional post skills made her a matchup nightmare and helped revolutionize the center position in women's basketball.

Drafted sixth overall by Washington in 2014, Dolson won a WNBA championship with the Mystics in 2019. In 2021, she made history by winning an Olympic gold medal in 3x3 basketball at the Tokyo Olympics, becoming the first openly gay player to win Olympic basketball gold. Known for her humor, advocacy, and clutch shooting, Dolson represents the modern center who can impact the game in multiple ways.
