üìñ Biography

Seimone Augustus is one of the smoothest and most elegant scorers in WNBA history. Known as "Money" for her clutch performances, she was the cornerstone of the Minnesota Lynx dynasty that won four championships in seven years.

üî• "When the game was on the line, you wanted the ball in Seimone's hands. Pure money."

Born on April 30, 1984, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, Augustus was a three-time National Player of the Year at LSU before being selected first overall by Minnesota in 2006. Her silky-smooth mid-range game and effortless scoring touch made her virtually unguardable.

Augustus won four WNBA championships with the Lynx (2011, 2013, 2015, 2017) and was named Finals MVP in 2011. An eight-time All-Star and six-time All-WNBA selection, she was also a three-time Olympic gold medalist (2008, 2012, 2016).

With 6,005 career points, Augustus ranks among the top 10 scorers in WNBA history. She retired in 2019 and was inducted into the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2024. Known for her professionalism and consistency, Augustus epitomized the complete wing player.
