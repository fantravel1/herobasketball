üìñ Biography

Ann Meyers shattered barriers throughout her career, becoming one of the most important figures in the history of women's basketball. She was the first woman to receive a full athletic scholarship to UCLA and later became the first woman to sign a contract with an NBA team.

"Ann Meyers opened doors that had never been opened before. Every female basketball player today owes her a debt of gratitude."

Born on March 26, 1955, in San Diego, California, Meyers came from an athletic family - her brother David Meyers won an NBA championship with the Milwaukee Bucks. At UCLA from 1974-1978, she became the first four-time All-American in women's basketball history.

In 1976, Meyers represented the United States at the Montreal Olympics, where women's basketball made its Olympic debut. She helped Team USA win the silver medal in that historic tournament.

In 1979, she made history again when she signed a contract with the Indiana Pacers, becoming the first woman to sign with an NBA team. Though she didn't make the final roster, her tryout was groundbreaking and received international attention.

After her playing career, Meyers became a successful broadcaster and sports executive. She married Hall of Fame baseball pitcher Don Drysdale in 1986 and later became the Vice President of the Phoenix Suns and Phoenix Mercury.

üéØ Post-Playing Career

Broadcaster: Meyers became a pioneering sports broadcaster, working for CBS, NBC, ESPN, and other networks covering basketball and other sports.

Executive: She served as Vice President of the Phoenix Suns and Phoenix Mercury, becoming one of the most powerful women executives in professional sports.

Advocate: Throughout her post-playing career, Meyers has been a tireless advocate for women's sports and Title IX, continuing to break barriers and open doors for future generations.
