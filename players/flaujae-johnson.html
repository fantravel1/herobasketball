üìñ Biography

Flau'jae Johnson is a unique two-way talent - a national championship-winning basketball player and a rising star in the rap world. The LSU guard helped lead the Tigers to the 2023 NCAA Championship as a freshman, playing a crucial role alongside Angel Reese in one of the most dominant teams in recent college basketball history.

Born on November 3, 2003, in Savannah, Georgia, Johnson is the daughter of rapper Camoflauge, who was killed when she was just six months old. She has honored his legacy by pursuing both basketball and music at the highest levels. Her performances on The Rap Game and America's Got Talent showcased her musical talent, while her play at LSU has demonstrated elite athletic ability.

As a junior in 2024-25, Johnson continues to develop as one of the SEC's premier two-way players. Her versatility, defensive intensity, and improved scoring make her a key piece of LSU's championship aspirations. Off the court, she's building a music career that includes deals with Jay-Z's Roc Nation, proving you can excel in multiple arenas.
