Elena Delle Donne is one of the most skilled and efficient scorers in WNBA history. The 6'5" forward won back-to-back WNBA MVP awards (2015, 2019) and led the Washington Mystics to their first championship in 2019.

Originally drafted #2 overall by Chicago in 2013, Delle Donne became the fastest player to 3,000 career points. She made history by becoming the first WNBA player to join the 50-40-90 club (50% FG, 40% 3PT, 90% FT) in a season. Her combination of size, shooting, and skill makes her one of the most versatile players ever.

Despite battling Lyme disease throughout her career, she has remained one of the league's premier players. Delle Donne attended Delaware, where she was a two-time All-American, after initially committing to UConn.
