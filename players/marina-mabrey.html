üìñ Biography

Marina Mabrey is an elite sharpshooter and versatile guard for the Connecticut Sun. Known for her deadly three-point shooting, offensive creativity, and ability to score in bunches, Mabrey has established herself as one of the league's most dangerous perimeter threats.

Born on September 14, 1996, in Belmar, New Jersey, Mabrey was a standout at the University of Notre Dame where she won the 2018 NCAA Championship alongside Brianna Turner and Jackie Young. Her shooting range and basketball IQ made her a highly valued prospect coming out of college.

Selected 19th overall by the Los Angeles Sparks in the 2019 WNBA Draft, Mabrey spent four seasons developing her game before joining the Chicago Sky and then the Connecticut Sun in 2024. Averaging 13.5 points per game with elite three-point shooting, she provides instant offense and spacing for the Sun while serving as a reliable scoring option in crucial moments.
