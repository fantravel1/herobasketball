Swin Cash is one of the most decorated winners in women's basketball history with four WNBA championships, two NCAA titles, and an Olympic gold medal. Her leadership and winning mentality made her invaluable wherever she played.

Cash won back-to-back NCAA championships at UConn (2000, 2002) before being drafted 2nd overall by Detroit in 2002. She won WNBA titles with the Shock (2003, 2006, 2008) and the New York Liberty (2016), becoming one of only a few players to win championships with multiple franchises.

Beyond her playing career, Cash has become a pioneering executive, currently serving as Vice President of Basketball Operations for the New Orleans Pelicans. She was inducted into the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2020 and continues to be a trailblazer for women in sports leadership.
