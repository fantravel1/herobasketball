Sylvia Fowles is one of the greatest centers in WNBA history and the league's all-time leading rebounder. The 6'6" dominant post player won four Defensive Player of the Year awards and two WNBA championships with the Minnesota Lynx (2017, 2018).

Fowles was the #2 overall pick in 2008 by Chicago before being traded to Minnesota in 2015, where she reached new heights. She won the 2017 MVP award and Finals MVP, becoming the first player to win both in the same season. An eight-time All-Star, she retired in 2022 as the league's all-time leader in rebounds.

Internationally, Fowles won two Olympic gold medals (2012, 2016) with Team USA. She played college basketball at LSU, where she was a two-time All-American and led the Tigers to three Final Fours.
