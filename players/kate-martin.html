üìñ Biography

Kate Martin went from Iowa Hawkeye fan favorite to WNBA champion with the Las Vegas Aces in her rookie season. The versatile guard/forward became one of the most beloved players in Iowa basketball history during her five-year career (2019-2024), known for her hustle, basketball IQ, and winning plays that don't always show up in the stat sheet.

Born on March 18, 2001, in Edwardsville, Illinois, Martin played a crucial role in Iowa's back-to-back national championship game appearances in 2023 and 2024 alongside Caitlin Clark. Her defensive versatility, rebounding, and ability to knock down big shots made her an essential piece of Iowa's historic run. She holds Iowa's record for career games played (168).

Drafted by the Las Vegas Aces in the 2nd round (18th overall) of the 2024 WNBA Draft, Martin earned a championship ring in her rookie season. Known for her infectious energy, team-first mentality, and ability to impact winning, she's building a professional career while maintaining a massive social media following from her college days.
