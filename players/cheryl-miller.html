üìñ Biography

Cheryl Miller is widely regarded as the greatest women's basketball player of the pre-WNBA era. Her dominance at USC from 1982-1986 revolutionized women's basketball and paved the way for the professional league that would follow.

"Before there was a WNBA, there was Cheryl Miller. She didn't just play the game - she transformed it."

Born on January 3, 1964, in Riverside, California, Miller was a basketball prodigy who once scored 105 points in a high school game. At USC, she led the Trojans to back-to-back NCAA Championships in 1983 and 1984, earning three consecutive Naismith Player of the Year awards.

Miller's athleticism was ahead of her time. She could dunk the basketball and brought a physical, above-the-rim style that had never been seen in women's basketball. Her combination of size (6'3"), skill, and competitive fire made her virtually unstoppable.

A knee injury cut short her playing career before she could join the WNBA, but her legacy was cemented. She was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 1995 and went on to coach the Phoenix Mercury (1997-2000) and work as a celebrated broadcaster for TNT.

She is the sister of NBA Hall of Famer Reggie Miller, and together they represent one of the greatest basketball families in history.

üéØ Post-Playing Career

Head Coach, Phoenix Mercury (1997-2000): Miller was one of the first WNBA head coaches, leading the Mercury in the league's inaugural seasons.

Broadcaster: After coaching, Miller became a celebrated basketball analyst for TNT, covering NBA games and becoming one of the most recognizable voices in basketball media.

Legacy: Miller's impact on women's basketball cannot be overstated. She proved that women's basketball could be exciting, athletic, and commercially viable, laying the groundwork for everything that followed.
