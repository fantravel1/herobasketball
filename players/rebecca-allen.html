üìñ Biography

Rebecca Allen is a versatile Australian guard/forward who has become an integral part of the New York Liberty's championship success. Known for her three-point shooting, defensive versatility, and high basketball IQ, Allen represents the modern wing player who can impact the game in multiple ways.

Born on November 2, 1992, in Melbourne, Victoria, Allen developed through Australia's elite basketball system, including time at the Australian Institute of Sport. Her combination of size, skill, and shooting ability made her a valuable international prospect who would eventually make her mark in the WNBA.

Selected 25th overall by the New York Liberty in the 2015 WNBA Draft, Allen spent her early career splitting time between the WNBA and Australia's WNBL. She eventually became a full-time contributor for the Liberty, providing crucial spacing, defense, and clutch shooting. In 2024, she played a key role in the Liberty's championship run, hitting important three-pointers throughout the playoffs.

A cornerstone of the Australian Opals, Allen won Olympic bronze in 2021 and has been a consistent performer in international competition. Her ability to guard multiple positions and knock down open threes has made her invaluable to both her WNBA and national teams. Allen continues to represent the growing influence of Australian players in the WNBA.
