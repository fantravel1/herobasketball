üìñ Biography

Jackie Young is a two-time WNBA champion and one of the fastest-rising stars in the league. As a key member of the Las Vegas Aces dynasty, Young has transformed from a promising rookie into an elite two-way guard who excels on both ends of the floor.

Born on September 16, 1997, in Princeton, Indiana, Young was a standout at the University of Notre Dame where she won a national championship in 2018 and was named the Final Four's Most Outstanding Player. Her versatility and high basketball IQ made her the first overall pick in the 2019 WNBA Draft.

Selected by the Las Vegas Aces, Young won back-to-back championships in 2022 and 2023, establishing herself as one of the league's premier guards. In 2024, she averaged career-highs of 17.8 points, 4.9 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game, earning All-Star honors and cementing her status as one of the WNBA's elite two-way players.
