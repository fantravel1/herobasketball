üìñ Biography

Brittney Griner is the most dominant post player in WNBA history and one of the most physically imposing athletes in basketball. At 6'9", she has revolutionized the center position with her combination of size, skill, and shot-blocking ability.

üí™ Resilience & Strength: After being wrongfully detained in Russia for nearly 10 months in 2022, BG returned to the WNBA in 2023 and earned her 10th All-Star selection. Her courage and perseverance have made her an inspiration far beyond basketball.

Born on October 18, 1990, in Houston, Texas, Griner dominated at Baylor University, where she led the Bears to an undefeated national championship in 2012 and was the all-time leader in blocks in NCAA history.

Selected 1st overall by the Phoenix Mercury in the 2013 WNBA Draft, Griner immediately became the league's most feared shot-blocker. She won a WNBA Championship in 2014 and has been selected to 10 All-Star teams.

On the international stage, Griner has won two Olympic Gold medals (2016, 2020) as a key member of Team USA, showcasing her dominance against the world's best competition.
