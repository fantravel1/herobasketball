üìñ Biography

Jewell Loyd is the heart and soul of the Seattle Storm franchise, carrying on the legacy of Seattle greats like Sue Bird and Lauren Jackson. Her explosive scoring ability and competitive fire have made her one of the premier guards in the WNBA.

üìä 2023 Scoring Champion: Loyd won her first scoring title in 2023, averaging 24.7 PPG. She became the first Storm player to lead the league in scoring since Lauren Jackson, cementing her status as Seattle's franchise player.

Born on October 5, 1993, in Lincolnwood, Illinois, Loyd starred at Notre Dame where she won two NCAA Championships (2014, 2015) alongside future WNBA stars. She was the 1st overall pick in the 2015 WNBA Draft by Seattle.

Loyd won WNBA Championships in 2018 and 2020, forming a dynamic duo with Breanna Stewart. After Stewart's departure, Loyd embraced the role of franchise cornerstone, leading the Storm's next chapter.

She won Olympic Gold with Team USA in Paris 2024 and continues to be one of the most exciting players to watch in the league.
