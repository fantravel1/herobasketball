üìñ Biography

Lisa Leslie is one of the most iconic figures in women's basketball history. Standing 6'5", she dominated as the premier center in the WNBA and became the first player to dunk in a WNBA game on July 30, 2002.

üî• "Lisa Leslie didn't just play above the rim. She changed what was possible for women in basketball."

Born on July 7, 1972, in Gardena, California, Leslie starred at USC before becoming a cornerstone of the Los Angeles Sparks franchise. She led the Sparks to back-to-back WNBA championships in 2001 and 2002, winning Finals MVP both times.

A three-time WNBA MVP (2001, 2004, 2006) and four-time Olympic gold medalist (1996, 2000, 2004, 2008), Leslie was a model of consistency and excellence. She was the first player to reach 3,000, 4,000, 5,000, and 6,000 career points in WNBA history.

Beyond basketball, Leslie became a successful businesswoman, sports analyst, and fashion model. She was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 2015 and the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2015, cementing her legacy as one of the game's all-time greats.
