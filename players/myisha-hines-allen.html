üìñ Biography

Myisha Hines-Allen is a versatile forward who has become a cornerstone of the Washington Mystics franchise. Known for her scoring ability, rebounding, and all-around game, Hines-Allen exemplifies the modern forward position with her ability to impact the game in multiple ways.

Born on February 17, 1996, in Washington D.C., Hines-Allen grew up in the nation's capital and became a local hero when she starred at the University of Louisville. At Louisville, she was an All-ACC selection and developed into one of the conference's most complete players, showcasing her scoring, rebounding, and passing abilities.

Drafted 8th overall by the Mystics in the 2018 WNBA Draft, Hines-Allen was part of the team's 2019 WNBA Championship run. Her breakout season came in 2020 when she won the WNBA's Most Improved Player award, averaging 15.4 points and 8.5 rebounds in the bubble season while becoming a focal point of the Mystics' offense.

Hines-Allen's ability to score from all three levels, crash the boards, and facilitate for teammates has made her an invaluable piece of the Mystics' core. Her hometown connection and championship pedigree have solidified her status as a franchise cornerstone in Washington.
