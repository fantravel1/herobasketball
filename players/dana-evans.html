üìñ Biography

Dana Evans is a sharpshooting guard and Louisville legend who has brought her championship pedigree and scoring touch to the Chicago Sky. Known for her deep three-point range, clutch gene, and leadership abilities, Evans is a true floor general.

Born on September 14, 1998, in Gary, Indiana, Evans became one of the most decorated players in Louisville history. During her college career, she led the Cardinals to the 2019 NCAA Championship and was named ACC Player of the Year as a senior. She finished her Louisville career as the program's all-time leading scorer with 2,312 points.

Evans' accolades at Louisville include three All-ACC First Team selections, making her one of the most accomplished players in conference history. Her ability to score from deep, run an offense, and deliver in clutch moments made her a fan favorite and one of the top prospects in the 2021 WNBA Draft.

Selected 13th overall by the Dallas Wings in 2021 and immediately traded to the Chicago Sky, Evans joined a championship-caliber team. She contributed to the Sky's 2021 WNBA Championship run and has continued to develop as a key rotation player, providing veteran leadership and three-point shooting to help the Sky compete at the highest level.
