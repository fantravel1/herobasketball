üìñ Biography

Candace Parker is one of the greatest basketball players of all time and a transformative figure in women's basketball. The two-time WNBA MVP, three-time champion, and two-time Olympic gold medalist redefined what was possible for women in basketball both on and off the court.

Born in St. Louis, Missouri, and raised in Naperville, Illinois, Parker became a legend at the University of Tennessee under Pat Summitt, winning back-to-back NCAA championships (2007, 2008) and becoming the first woman to dunk in an NCAA tournament game. As the first overall pick in the 2008 WNBA Draft, she became the first player to win Rookie of the Year and MVP in the same season.

Parker's 16-year WNBA career included stints with the Los Angeles Sparks (2008-2020), Chicago Sky (2021-2022), and Las Vegas Aces (2023-2024), winning championships in different cities and becoming the first player to win Finals MVP with multiple teams. Her versatility, basketball IQ, and ability to play all five positions made her one of the most unique talents the game has ever seen. Since retiring in 2024, she has transitioned seamlessly into broadcasting as an NBA analyst for TNT and NBA TV, breaking barriers as one of the first women to serve as a full-time NBA game analyst.
