Teresa Edwards is the most decorated American basketball player in Olympic history. She competed in five Olympic Games (1984-2000), winning four gold medals and one bronze, a record that stands unmatched.

Edwards played college basketball at Georgia, winning two SEC championships. She spent most of her professional career overseas, where she dominated European leagues before the WNBA was founded. When the ABL and WNBA launched, she briefly played professionally in the United States.

As a point guard, Edwards was known for her leadership, court vision, and competitive fire. She was the first flag bearer for USA Basketball at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics and was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2011. She remains an iconic figure who paved the way for future generations of women's basketball players.
