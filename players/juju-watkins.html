üìñ Biography

JuJu Watkins took the college basketball world by storm as a freshman at USC, averaging 27.1 points per game in 2023-24 - one of the highest scoring averages by a freshman in NCAA history. The Los Angeles native became an instant star, selling out arenas and drawing national television audiences as she transformed the USC program.

Born on July 15, 2005, in Los Angeles, California, Watkins (full name Judea Watkins) arrived at USC as the consensus #1 recruit in her class. She lived up to the hype immediately, scoring 32 points in her college debut and never looking back. Her combination of size, athleticism, scoring ability, and basketball IQ make her a matchup nightmare for opponents.

As a sophomore in 2024-25, Watkins is establishing herself as one of the premier players in women's basketball. Playing in her hometown, she's helping revive USC women's basketball while building a resume that has her projected as a future top WNBA draft pick. Her impact extends beyond statistics - she's bringing unprecedented attention to West Coast women's basketball.
