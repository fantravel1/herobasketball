üìñ Biography

Nancy Lieberman, known as "Lady Magic" for her exceptional playmaking abilities, is one of the most influential figures in basketball history. A revolutionary point guard, she broke barriers as both a player and coach throughout her remarkable career.

"Nancy Lieberman didn't just play point guard - she redefined what a point guard could be in women's basketball."

Born on July 1, 1958, in Brooklyn, New York, Lieberman grew up playing basketball in the parks of Harlem, developing the toughness and skills that would make her a legend. At just 18 years old, she became the youngest basketball player in Olympic history when she won a silver medal at the 1976 Montreal Olympics.

At Old Dominion University, she led the Monarchs to back-to-back AIAW National Championships in 1979 and 1980, winning consecutive Wade Trophies as the nation's best player. Her court vision, passing ability, and competitive fire earned her the nickname "Lady Magic."

Lieberman continued to break barriers throughout her career. She played in the USBL (a men's league) and later became the first woman to coach a men's professional basketball team when she took the helm of the Texas Legends in the NBA G-League.

She was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 1996 and continues to be an advocate for women's basketball and sports equality.

üéØ Post-Playing Career

Head Coach, Texas Legends (2009-2011): Lieberman made history as the first woman to serve as head coach of a men's professional basketball team in the NBA Development League (now G-League).

Assistant Coach, Sacramento Kings (2015-2017): She became the second female assistant coach in NBA history, working under George Karl and Dave Joerger.

Broadcaster & Analyst: A respected basketball analyst who has worked for ESPN and other networks, sharing her expertise and promoting women's basketball.

Advocate: Throughout her career, Lieberman has been a tireless advocate for women in sports, using her platform to inspire future generations of players and coaches.
