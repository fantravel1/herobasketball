üìñ Biography

Steph Talbot is the captain of the Australian Opals and a cornerstone of Australian women's basketball. The Adelaide native has dedicated her career to representing her country with pride, becoming one of the most respected leaders in international basketball.

Born on June 15, 1994, in Adelaide, South Australia, Talbot grew up in basketball, with her father Ray Talbot having played professionally. She developed through Australia's elite pathways, including the Australian Institute of Sport, before making her mark in the WNBL with her hometown Adelaide Lightning.

Talbot has had stints in the WNBA with Phoenix Mercury, Minnesota Lynx, and most recently the Los Angeles Sparks, but she's best known for her international exploits. As captain of the Opals, she led Australia to Olympic bronze in 2021 and has been a consistent performer at World Cups and continental championships. Her defensive intensity, versatility, and leadership make her invaluable.

A multiple-time WNBL champion and MVP with Adelaide Lightning, Talbot has chosen to focus on the Australian league while leading her national team. Her commitment to Australian basketball, work ethic, and team-first mentality have made her a role model for aspiring players. As Opals captain, she carries the responsibility of continuing Australia's proud basketball tradition.
