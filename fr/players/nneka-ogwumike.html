üìñ Biography

Nneka Ogwumike is one of the most accomplished and influential players in WNBA history, excelling both on the court as a champion and MVP, and off it as the long-time president of the WNBA Players Association.

The 2016 MVP led the Los Angeles Sparks to that year's championship, hitting the game-winning shot in Game 5 of the Finals. After 12 seasons in LA, she joined the Seattle Storm in 2024, reuniting with fellow Stanford alum Skylar Diggins-Smith.

As WNBPA President since 2016, Ogwumike has been instrumental in negotiating landmark CBA agreements that dramatically improved player salaries and benefits. Her advocacy has helped transform the business of women's basketball.

One of four Ogwumike sisters to play professionally (including Chiney), Nneka represents basketball royalty and continues to inspire the next generation.
