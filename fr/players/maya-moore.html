Maya Moore is one of the most accomplished players in basketball history and a transformative social justice advocate. She walked away from basketball at the peak of her powers to fight for criminal justice reform‚Äîand won.

‚úä "Maya Moore showed the world that some victories matter more than championships."

Born on June 11, 1989, in Jefferson City, Missouri, Moore dominated at every level. She won two NCAA championships at UConn under Geno Auriemma before being selected first overall by Minnesota in 2011. In her rookie season, she led the Lynx to the championship and won Finals MVP.

Moore won four WNBA titles (2011, 2013, 2015, 2017), the 2014 MVP award, and two Olympic gold medals (2012, 2016). At her peak in 2019, she stepped away from basketball to advocate for Jonathan Irons, a man wrongly convicted of burglary. Her efforts led to his release in 2020, and the two later married.

Moore announced her retirement in 2023, choosing social justice work over a potential Hall of Fame extension. Her legacy transcends basketball‚Äîshe proved that athletes can be powerful agents of change. She remains one of only seven players to win Rookie of the Year, MVP, Finals MVP, and Olympic gold.
