üìñ Biography

A'ja Wilson is one of the most dominant players in WNBA history. Standing at 6'4" (193 cm), the Las Vegas Aces center has established herself as the face of women's basketball with back-to-back MVP awards and two WNBA championships.

Born on August 8, 1996, in Hopkins, South Carolina, Wilson attended Heathwood Hall Episcopal School before committing to the University of South Carolina. Under head coach Dawn Staley, she became a two-time SEC Player of the Year and led the Gamecocks to their first national championship in 2017.

Selected first overall by the Las Vegas Aces in the 2018 WNBA Draft, Wilson has transformed the franchise into a dynasty. Her combination of elite scoring, rebounding, and shot-blocking makes her one of the most complete players the league has ever seen.
