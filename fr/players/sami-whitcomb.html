üìñ Biography

Sami Whitcomb is one of the WNBA's most lethal three-point shooters and a beloved member of the Seattle Storm. Born in California but representing Australia internationally, Whitcomb has carved out an impressive career as a sharpshooter and clutch performer.

Born on July 20, 1988, in Ventura, California, Whitcomb played college basketball at the University of Washington, where she became one of the Huskies' all-time great shooters. After going undrafted, she pursued professional opportunities overseas, playing in Australia's WNBL where her performances caught the attention of both scouts and the Australian national team.

Whitcomb's connection to Australia through family led her to represent the Opals internationally, and she eventually earned Australian citizenship. In 2017, she joined the Seattle Storm and quickly became a fan favorite for her shooting ability and infectious energy. She won a WNBA championship with Seattle in 2018 and captured the WNBA Three-Point Contest in 2019.

A key member of the Australian Opals, Whitcomb won Olympic bronze in 2021 and has been a reliable shooter in international competition. Her journey from undrafted to WNBA champion exemplifies perseverance and determination. Known for her quick release and deep range, Whitcomb continues to be a valuable weapon off the bench for Seattle while representing Australia with pride.
