üìñ Biography

Chelsea Gray is the maestro of the Las Vegas Aces dynasty, orchestrating one of the most potent offenses in WNBA history. Her court vision, mid-range mastery, and clutch performances have made her one of the most respected point guards in the game.

The 2022 Finals MVP, Gray was unstoppable in Las Vegas's first championship run, averaging 18.3 PPG in the Finals while picking apart the Connecticut Sun defense with surgical precision.

Born on October 8, 1992, Gray played at Duke before being drafted by Connecticut in 2014. After stints with the Sun and Los Angeles Sparks, she joined Las Vegas in 2021 and immediately became the missing piece for a championship run.

Known for her signature floater in the lane and ability to control the pace of the game, Gray is the steadying presence that allows A'ja Wilson and Kelsey Plum to thrive.
