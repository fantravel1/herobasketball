üìñ Biography

Cynthia Cooper, known as "Coop," was the original WNBA superstar and the driving force behind the Houston Comets dynasty that won the league's first four championships (1997-2000). She dominated the early years of the WNBA like no other player.

‚≠ê "Cynthia Cooper didn't just win championships. She defined what a WNBA superstar looked like."

Born on April 14, 1963, in Chicago, Illinois, Cooper starred at USC before spending over a decade playing professionally in Europe. At age 34, when the WNBA launched in 1997, she finally had a chance to showcase her talents on home soil‚Äîand she didn't waste it.

Cooper won back-to-back MVP awards (1997, 1998) and an unprecedented four consecutive Finals MVP awards (1997-2000). She led the league in scoring in its first three seasons, averaging an astounding 22.2 PPG in 1997. Her clutch performances and scoring ability set the standard for excellence.

After retiring in 2000 (and a brief comeback in 2003), Cooper was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 2010 and the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2009. She also won Olympic gold in 1988 and served as a WNBA head coach. Her legacy as the league's first true superstar remains unmatched.
