üìñ Biography

Gabby Williams is a French-American basketball star who embodies the modern two-way player. Born in Sparks, Nevada, with French heritage through her mother, Williams has become a cornerstone of French basketball while maintaining a successful WNBA career.

Williams starred at the University of Connecticut, winning an NCAA championship in 2016 as part of the Huskies' storied program. Her versatility, defensive prowess, and basketball IQ made her a first-round WNBA draft pick in 2018, selected 4th overall by the Chicago Sky.

After winning a WNBA championship with Chicago in 2021, Williams joined the Seattle Storm and has become an integral part of their system. Her ability to guard multiple positions, create off the dribble, and make winning plays has made her invaluable in critical moments.

Williams' decision to represent France internationally has elevated Les Bleues to new heights. She was instrumental in France's historic silver medal at the 2024 Paris Olympics, playing lockdown defense and providing crucial scoring. Her dual-continent career continues to inspire players with multicultural backgrounds, and she remains a key figure in European basketball while starring in the WNBA.
