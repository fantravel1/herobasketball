The Los Angeles Sparks are one of the WNBA's original eight franchises, founded in 1997. With three championships and a legacy of legendary players, the Sparks have helped define women's professional basketball.

The early 2000s dynasty featured Lisa Leslie, the first woman to dunk in a WNBA game, leading back-to-back championships in 2001 and 2002. Candace Parker later brought a third title in 2016 with a Finals MVP performance against the Minnesota Lynx.

Currently rebuilding with young talent like Rickea Jackson and veteran leader Dearica Hamby, the Sparks are working to return to championship contention. Playing in Hollywood, the franchise continues to attract attention and cultivate the next generation of stars.
