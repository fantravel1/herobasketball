Reference

Basketball Glossary

100+ basketball terms, stats, and definitions explained. From basic concepts to advanced analytics.

ABCDFPRST
A
Assist AST
A pass that directly leads to a made basket by a teammate. The passer is credited with an assist.
Example: Caitlin Clark led the WNBA with 8.4 APG in 2024.
APG
Assists Per Game. The average number of assists a player records per game played.
Alley-Oop
A play where one player throws the ball near the basket and a teammate catches it mid-air and scores, usually with a dunk or layup.
B
Block BLK
When a defensive player legally deflects a shot attempt, preventing it from reaching the basket.
Example: A'ja Wilson averaged 2.6 blocks per game in her MVP season.
BPG
Blocks Per Game. The average number of blocked shots a player records per game.
Box Out
A rebounding technique where a player positions their body between an opponent and the basket to gain better position for a rebound.
C
Center C
Typically the tallest player on the team, playing near the basket on offense and defense. Responsible for rebounding, rim protection, and interior scoring.
Crossover
A dribbling move where the ball handler quickly switches the ball from one hand to the other in front of their body to change direction.
D
Double-Double
When a player reaches double digits in two statistical categories in a single game (e.g., 20 points and 10 rebounds).
DPOY
Defensive Player of the Year. Annual award given to the best defensive player in the league.
F
Field Goal FG
Any basket scored during regular play, including two-pointers and three-pointers (excluding free throws).
FG%
Field Goal Percentage. The ratio of made field goals to attempted field goals, expressed as a percentage.
Formula: (FGM / FGA) √ó 100
Free Throw FT
An uncontested shot taken from the free throw line (15 feet from the basket), worth one point. Awarded after certain fouls.
P
PPG
Points Per Game. The average number of points a player scores per game played.
Example: A'ja Wilson led the WNBA with 26.9 PPG in 2024.
PER
Player Efficiency Rating. An all-in-one advanced stat that measures a player's per-minute productivity. League average is 15.
Point Guard PG
The primary ball handler and playmaker on the team. Responsible for running the offense, calling plays, and distributing the ball.
Pick and Roll
An offensive play where a player sets a screen (pick) for the ball handler, then moves toward the basket (roll) to receive a pass.
R
Rebound REB
Gaining possession of the ball after a missed shot. Can be offensive (after your team's miss) or defensive (after opponent's miss).
RPG
Rebounds Per Game. The average number of rebounds a player grabs per game.
S
Steal STL
When a defensive player takes the ball away from an offensive player through legal means.
SPG
Steals Per Game. The average number of steals a player records per game.
T
Triple-Double
When a player reaches double digits in three statistical categories in a single game (e.g., 15 points, 12 rebounds, 10 assists).
Example: Sabrina Ionescu holds the NCAA record with 26 career triple-doubles.
Three-Pointer 3P
A field goal made from beyond the three-point arc, worth three points. The WNBA three-point line is 22 feet 1.75 inches at the top.
Turnover TO
When the offensive team loses possession of the ball to the defense without attempting a shot.
