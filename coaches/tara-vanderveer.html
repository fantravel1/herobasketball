üìñ About Tara VanDerveer

Tara VanDerveer retired in April 2024 as the all-time winningest coach in women's college basketball history with 1,216 victories. Over 38 seasons at Stanford, she built one of the most consistently excellent programs in the nation, winning three national championships (1990, 1992, 2021) and making 13 Final Four appearances.

VanDerveer's impact on the sport extends beyond Stanford. She took a leave of absence in 1995-96 to coach the U.S. Women's National Team, leading them to an Olympic gold medal at the 1996 Atlanta Games. That team's success helped launch the WNBA and elevated women's basketball to new heights of popularity.

In December 2020, she surpassed Pat Summitt's record of 1,098 wins to become the all-time leader. Three months later, she led Stanford to its first national championship since 1992. Known for her meticulous preparation and player development, VanDerveer sent dozens of players to the WNBA and helped shape the careers of coaches throughout the sport.
