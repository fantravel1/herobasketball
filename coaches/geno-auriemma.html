üìñ About Geno Auriemma

Geno Auriemma is the most successful coach in women's college basketball history, having won 11 NCAA Championships at the University of Connecticut. Since arriving in Storrs in 1985, Auriemma has transformed UConn into the most dominant dynasty in the sport.

Born in Italy and raised in Pennsylvania, Auriemma built UConn from a program with no history into the gold standard of women's basketball. His teams have set records for excellence, including an unprecedented 111-game winning streak (2014-2017) and four consecutive national championships (2013-2016).

Auriemma has coached four Olympic gold medal teams for USA Basketball and produced more WNBA All-Stars than any other college program. His coaching tree includes numerous successful coaches at both the college and professional levels. He was inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2006.
