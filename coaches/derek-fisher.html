üìñ About Derek Fisher

Derek Fisher made history when he became the first former NBA player to serve as a WNBA head coach. Appointed by the Los Angeles Sparks in 2019, Fisher brought his championship pedigree and basketball IQ to the women's game, bridging the gap between the NBA and WNBA in a groundbreaking way.

Fisher's playing career was legendary. As the starting point guard for the Los Angeles Lakers, he won five NBA Championships (2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, 2010) alongside Kobe Bryant and Shaquille O'Neal. Known for clutch shooting and leadership, Fisher played 18 seasons in the NBA and served as president of the NBA Players Association.

His transition to WNBA coaching represents an important moment in the convergence of men's and women's professional basketball. Fisher has brought his championship experience, attention to detail, and understanding of what it takes to win at the highest level to the Sparks organization, working to restore the franchise to its championship glory.
