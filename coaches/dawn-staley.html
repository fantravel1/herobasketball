üìñ About Dawn Staley

Dawn Staley is the head women's basketball coach at the University of South Carolina, where she has built one of the most dominant programs in NCAA history. Since taking over in 2008, Staley has transformed South Carolina into a national powerhouse, winning three NCAA National Championships (2017, 2022, 2024).

Before her coaching career, Staley was one of the greatest point guards in women's basketball history. She won three Olympic gold medals as a player (1996, 2000, 2004) and was named to the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame in 2013. She played in the WNBA for the Charlotte Sting, Houston Comets, and Sacramento Monarchs.

As USA Basketball's head coach, Staley led Team USA to Olympic gold at the Tokyo 2020 and Paris 2024 Olympics, continuing America's dominant streak in women's basketball. Her 2024 South Carolina team completed an undefeated 38-0 season, cementing her status as one of the greatest coaches in women's basketball history.
