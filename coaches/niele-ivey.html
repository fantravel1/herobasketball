üìñ About Niele Ivey

Niele Ivey is the head women's basketball coach at the University of Notre Dame, her alma mater where she was a standout point guard. As a player, she led Notre Dame to the 2001 NCAA National Championship and was named to the All-Big East First Team.

Before returning to Notre Dame as head coach in 2020, Ivey had a diverse coaching career that included serving as an assistant coach in the NBA with the Memphis Grizzlies (2019-2020), making her one of the few women to coach at that level. She also served as an assistant at Notre Dame under Muffet McGraw from 2007-2014 and 2017-2019.

Ivey is known for her ability to develop talent and her deep connection to the Notre Dame program. Her son, Jaden Ivey, is an NBA player for the Detroit Pistons, making basketball a true family tradition. Under her leadership, Notre Dame continues to compete at the highest levels of women's college basketball.
