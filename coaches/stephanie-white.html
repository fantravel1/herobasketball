üìñ About Stephanie White

Stephanie White is the head coach of the Connecticut Sun, bringing a wealth of championship experience as both a player and coach. An Indiana basketball legend, White won an NCAA championship with Purdue in 2000 and a WNBA championship as an assistant coach with the Indiana Fever in 2012.

White's playing career was distinguished by her exceptional court vision and leadership. After her playing days, she transitioned seamlessly into coaching and broadcasting. She served as head coach of the Indiana Fever from 2015-2016, earning WNBA Coach of the Year honors in 2016 after leading the team to the WNBA Finals. Following her time with the Fever, she worked as an analyst for various networks while coaching at Vanderbilt University.

White returned to the WNBA as head coach of the Connecticut Sun in 2023, bringing her championship pedigree to one of the league's most competitive franchises. Her deep understanding of the game from both playing and coaching perspectives, combined with her ability to connect with players, makes her one of the most respected coaches in women's basketball. She is set to return to the Indiana Fever as head coach in 2025.
