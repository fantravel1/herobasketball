üìñ About Kim Mulkey

Kim Mulkey is one of the most decorated coaches in women's college basketball history. With four national championships across two programs (Baylor and LSU), she has established herself as a dynasty builder and one of the sport's most polarizing and successful figures.

Before her coaching career, Mulkey was an elite player. She won two national championships as a point guard at Louisiana Tech (1981, 1982) and an Olympic gold medal with Team USA in 1984. Her playing career gave her a foundation of excellence that translated into coaching.

At Baylor, Mulkey built the program from scratch, winning three national championships (2005, 2012, 2019). In 2021, she returned to her home state to coach LSU, and in just her second season, led the Tigers to their first national championship in program history (2023), defeating Iowa and Caitlin Clark in a record-setting final.
