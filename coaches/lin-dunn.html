üìñ About Lin Dunn

Lin Dunn is one of the true pioneers of women's basketball coaching, with a career spanning over four decades. She led the Indiana Fever to their first WNBA Championship in 2012, capping a remarkable coaching journey that began long before the WNBA existed.

Dunn was an original WNBA coach, serving as the first head coach of the Seattle Storm (2000-2002) after previous stints with the Portland Power (ABL) and various college programs. Her ability to develop players and build winning cultures made her one of the most respected figures in women's basketball.

After retiring in 2014, Dunn returned to coaching in 2024 when the Indiana Fever, now led by rookie sensation Caitlin Clark, needed an experienced hand. Her return demonstrates her enduring passion for the game and her commitment to women's basketball. She was inducted into the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2014.
