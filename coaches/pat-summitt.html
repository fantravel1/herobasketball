üìñ The Legend of Pat Summitt

Patricia Sue Summitt is universally regarded as the greatest coach in women's basketball history. From 1974 to 2012, she led the Tennessee Lady Volunteers to unprecedented success, winning eight national championships and finishing with a remarkable 1,098 career wins‚Äîmore than any other Division I basketball coach, male or female, at the time of her retirement.

Summitt's impact extended far beyond wins and championships. She had a 100% graduation rate for players who completed their eligibility at Tennessee. Her "definite dozen" rules of life taught discipline, accountability, and excellence. She mentored countless women who went on to become coaches, including many current head coaches in college basketball.

In 2011, Summitt was diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer's disease. Rather than stepping away, she continued coaching for one more season, winning SEC Coach of the Year. After retiring in 2012, she established the Pat Summitt Foundation to fight Alzheimer's. She passed away in 2016, leaving behind a legacy of excellence, integrity, and inspiration.

In 2012, President Barack Obama awarded her the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the nation's highest civilian honor, recognizing her contributions to women's sports and her courage in facing Alzheimer's disease publicly.
