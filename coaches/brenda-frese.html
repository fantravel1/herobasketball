üìñ About Brenda Frese

Brenda Frese has transformed the University of Maryland into one of the premier programs in women's college basketball. Since taking over in 2002, she has led the Terrapins to a national championship in 2006 and consistently competed for conference titles in both the ACC and Big Ten.

Frese's 2006 championship team featured Crystal Langhorne and Marissa Coleman, defeating Duke in the national title game. The victory established Maryland as a national power and solidified Frese's reputation as one of the best coaches in the country.

Known for her high-tempo offense and ability to develop players, Frese has sent numerous players to the WNBA and earned seven Big Ten Coach of the Year awards. She is one of the highest-paid women's basketball coaches in the nation, earning approximately $1.9 million annually.
