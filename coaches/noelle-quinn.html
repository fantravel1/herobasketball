üìñ About Noelle Quinn

Noelle Quinn is the head coach of the Seattle Storm, a position she's held since 2021. A former WNBA player who won two championships with Seattle (2018, 2020), Quinn brings a unique perspective to coaching as someone who helped build the Storm's winning culture as both a player and assistant coach.

As a player, Quinn spent 13 seasons in the WNBA, including seven with the Seattle Storm. She was known for her defensive intensity, versatility, and basketball IQ. After retiring as a player, she immediately transitioned to the Storm's coaching staff as an assistant before being promoted to head coach in 2021, making her one of the youngest head coaches in WNBA history.

Quinn's coaching philosophy emphasizes the same principles that defined her playing career: relentless defense, team-first mentality, and a commitment to excellence. She continues the Storm's tradition of developing young talent while maintaining championship aspirations. Her seamless transition from player to coach demonstrates her deep understanding of the game and her ability to connect with players on and off the court.
