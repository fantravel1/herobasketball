üìñ About Kara Lawson

Kara Lawson is one of the most accomplished women to ever transition from playing to coaching at the highest levels. She became the head coach at Duke University in 2020 after a decorated career as a player, broadcaster, and NBA assistant coach.

As a player, Lawson won a WNBA Championship with the Sacramento Monarchs (2005) and an Olympic gold medal with Team USA (2008). She was a three-time WNBA All-Star and played 12 seasons in the league. After retiring, she became one of the most respected analysts in basketball, working for ESPN.

Before Duke, Lawson served as an assistant coach with the Boston Celtics, becoming one of the few women to coach in the NBA. At Duke, she is building the program with a focus on player development and competing in one of college basketball's toughest conferences, the ACC.
