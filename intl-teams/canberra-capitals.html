üìñ Club History

Canberra Capitals is the most decorated franchise in WNBL history with 8 championships. Based in Australia's capital city, the Caps have been a powerhouse of Australian women's basketball since their founding in 1984.

The club's legacy is intertwined with Australian basketball legend Lauren Jackson, who began her professional career with the Capitals before becoming a three-time WNBA MVP. The team has consistently produced Australian Opals national team players and has been a destination for international talent.

The Capitals experienced a dynasty period in the early 2000s, winning multiple consecutive championships. Their commitment to developing local talent while remaining competitive has made them a model franchise in Australian basketball.
