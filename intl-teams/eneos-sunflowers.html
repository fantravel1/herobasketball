üìñ Club History

ENEOS Sunflowers is the most successful club in Japanese women's basketball history, with an unmatched 21 WJBL championships. Originally known as JOMO Sunflowers, the team has been the standard-bearer for excellence in Japanese women's basketball for over seven decades.

The club's corporate backing from ENEOS (Japan's largest oil company) has provided the resources to attract and develop top Japanese talent, as well as occasional international stars. Their distinctive orange and yellow colors are instantly recognizable across Japan.

ENEOS has been a primary pipeline for Japan's national team, contributing numerous players to Olympic rosters. The team's technical style and emphasis on ball movement has influenced Japanese basketball philosophy. The club's 2020 Tokyo Olympics contributions helped Japan win the silver medal.
