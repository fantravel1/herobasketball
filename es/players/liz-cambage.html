üìñ Biograf√≠a

Liz Cambage is one of the most dominant centers in women's basketball history and holds the WNBA single-game scoring record with an incredible 53-point performance. The Australian superstar has been a force on both the WNBA and international stages, known for her scoring prowess, shot-blocking ability, and imposing presence.

Born on August 18, 1991, in London to an Australian mother and Nigerian father, Cambage moved to Australia as a child and developed into one of the country's greatest basketball exports. At 6'8" (203 cm), she combines rare size with exceptional skill, able to dominate in the post while also stepping out for mid-range jumpers.

Selected second overall by the Tulsa Shock in the 2011 WNBA Draft, Cambage made history on July 17, 2018, when she scored 53 points for the Dallas Wings against the New York Liberty, breaking the previous WNBA single-game scoring record. She shot an efficient 17-of-22 from the field in that historic performance.

A cornerstone of the Australian Opals, Cambage helped Australia win Olympic bronze in 2012 and has been a dominant force in international competition. She's also starred in the WNBL (Australia's domestic league) and in China, earning championships and MVP honors. Her combination of talent, personality, and advocacy for mental health awareness has made her one of basketball's most recognizable figures.
