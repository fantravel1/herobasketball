üìñ Biography

Betnijah Laney-Hamilton is a versatile guard whose journey from overlooked prospect to WNBA All-Star exemplifies perseverance and continuous improvement. The Los Angeles Sparks veteran brings elite two-way play, veteran leadership, and championship experience to her hometown team.

Born in Columbus, Ohio, and raised in Southern California, Laney-Hamilton starred at Rutgers before being drafted 17th overall in 2015. After early career struggles, she broke through with the Atlanta Dream, winning the 2020 WNBA Most Improved Player award and earning All-WNBA Second Team honors with a career-high 17.2 points per game.

Her success with Atlanta earned her a move to the New York Liberty, where she continued her All-Star caliber play before returning home to Los Angeles in 2024. Known for her defensive versatility, mid-range game, and clutch performances, Laney-Hamilton has become one of the league's most complete and respected two-way players.
