üìñ Biography

Tina Thompson was the first player ever drafted in WNBA history, selected first overall by the Houston Comets in 1997. She became the cornerstone of the most dominant dynasty in WNBA history, winning four consecutive championships from 1997-2000.

üèÜ "Tina Thompson didn't just start the WNBA. She defined excellence for an entire generation."

Born on February 10, 1975, in Los Angeles, California, Thompson starred at USC before making history as the first WNBA draft pick. Her smooth scoring touch, mid-range game, and basketball IQ made her one of the most complete forwards of her era.

By the time she retired in 2013, Thompson was the WNBA's all-time leading scorer with 7,488 points, a record that stood until being broken by Diana Taurasi. A nine-time All-Star and eight-time All-WNBA selection, she was also a two-time Olympic gold medalist (2004, 2008).

Thompson was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 2018 and the Women's Basketball Hall of Fame in 2018. She now serves as head coach at the University of Virginia, passing on her championship pedigree to the next generation.
