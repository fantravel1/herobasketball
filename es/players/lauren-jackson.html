üìñ Biography

Lauren Jackson is widely regarded as the greatest international player in WNBA history and one of the most dominant forces the league has ever seen. The Australian legend combined size, skill, and versatility like few others.

üåè "Lauren Jackson showed the world that basketball excellence knows no borders."

Born on May 11, 1981, in Albury, New South Wales, Jackson dominated Australian basketball before being selected first overall by Seattle in 2001. At 6'5", she could score from anywhere on the court, combining post moves with a deadly three-point shot.

A three-time WNBA MVP (2003, 2007, 2010) and two-time champion (2004, 2010), Jackson was the most unstoppable scorer of her era. She led the league in scoring three times and made seven All-Star teams. Her 6,007 career points rank among the all-time greats.

Jackson's international resume is equally impressive: three Olympic medals (three silver 2000, 2004, 2008; one bronze 2012) and multiple FIBA World Cup medals. She was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 2021. After retiring due to injuries, she made a remarkable comeback to play in the 2022 FIBA World Cup at age 41.
