üìñ Biography

Diana Taurasi is universally regarded as the greatest women's basketball player of all time. Known as the "White Mamba" and "The GOAT," she has dominated every level of basketball for over two decades.

üêê "There will never be another Diana Taurasi. She changed the game forever."

Born on June 11, 1982, in Glendale, California, Taurasi led UConn to three consecutive NCAA championships (2002-2004) before being selected first overall by the Phoenix Mercury in 2004. She has been the face of the Mercury franchise ever since.

Taurasi holds the WNBA record for career points (10,518) and has won virtually every award the sport has to offer: MVP, Finals MVP, scoring titles, and more Olympic gold medals (5) than any other basketball player in history.

At 42 years old, she continues to compete at the highest level, proving that greatness knows no age limit. Her competitive fire, clutch performances, and legendary trash talk have made her an icon of the sport.
