Emma Meesseman is one of Europe's greatest basketball exports and a Belgian national hero. The versatile center/forward has dominated on both sides of the Atlantic, winning championships in the WNBA and EuroLeague while becoming the face of Belgian women's basketball.

Born on May 13, 1993, in Ypres, Belgium, Meesseman developed her unique skill set in European basketball before being drafted 19th overall by the Washington Mystics in 2013. Her ability to score from anywhere on the court, combined with exceptional court vision and basketball IQ, makes her one of the most complete players in the world.

Meesseman's crowning achievement came in 2019 when she led the Washington Mystics to their first WNBA championship, earning Finals MVP honors with dominant performances. She's also a multiple-time EuroLeague champion and led Belgium to a historic fourth-place finish at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, cementing her status as one of the game's international legends.

Known for her humility and team-first approach, Meesseman splits her time between the WNBA and top European clubs, showcasing her talents in Turkey's powerhouse league while remaining the cornerstone of the Belgian Cats national team.
