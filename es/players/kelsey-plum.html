üìñ Biography

Kelsey Plum is one of the most electrifying scorers in the WNBA and a key piece of the Las Vegas Aces dynasty. Her smooth shooting stroke, crafty ball-handling, and clutch gene have made her a fan favorite and a champion.

üéì NCAA All-Time Leading Scorer: At Washington, Plum became the all-time leading scorer in NCAA Division I women's basketball history with 3,527 points. Her senior season (2016-17) saw her average 31.7 PPG, the highest in NCAA history.

Born on August 24, 1994, in Poway, California, Plum was the #1 overall pick in the 2017 WNBA Draft by San Antonio. After the franchise relocated to Las Vegas, she became a cornerstone of the Aces' championship teams.

Plum won back-to-back WNBA Championships in 2022 and 2023, while also capturing the 2022 All-Star Game MVP award. She's represented Team USA at the Olympics, winning gold in 3x3 basketball (2020) and 5x5 basketball (2024).

Known for her infectious energy and social media presence, Plum has become one of the most marketable players in the WNBA.
