üìñ Biography

Sue Bird is the greatest point guard in women's basketball history. Over a 21-year career with the Seattle Storm, she redefined the position and built a legacy unmatched in the sport.

‚ö° "Sue Bird's basketball IQ and leadership were transcendent. She made everyone around her better."

Born on October 16, 1980, in Syosset, New York, Bird won two NCAA championships at UConn (2000, 2002) before being selected first overall by Seattle in 2002. She would spend her entire WNBA career with the Storm, winning four championships.

Bird holds the WNBA record for career assists (3,234) and is tied for the most Olympic gold medals in basketball history (5). Her partnership with Diana Taurasi on Team USA produced unprecedented international dominance.

She retired after the 2022 season as one of only seven players in WNBA history to play 500+ games, all with the same franchise. Her number 10 was retired by the Storm in 2023.
