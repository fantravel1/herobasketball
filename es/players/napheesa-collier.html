üìñ Biography

Napheesa Collier has emerged as one of the WNBA's brightest stars and the centerpiece of the Minnesota Lynx's championship aspirations. Her all-around game, combining scoring, rebounding, playmaking, and defense, makes her one of the most complete players in the league.

üèÜ 2024 MVP Runner-Up: Collier finished second in MVP voting in 2024, leading the Lynx to the best record in the Western Conference. She's firmly established herself as one of the top 3 players in the WNBA.

Born on September 23, 1996, in O'Fallon, Missouri, Collier was a four-time All-American at UConn, winning two NCAA Championships (2015, 2016) under Geno Auriemma. She was the 6th overall pick in the 2019 WNBA Draft.

After winning Rookie of the Year in 2019, Collier has steadily improved each season. The 2024 season marked her ascent to superstar status, as she carried Minnesota to a 30-win season and was named to her fourth consecutive All-Star team.

She won an Olympic Gold Medal with Team USA in Tokyo 2020 and continues to represent American basketball at the highest level.

üåü Unrivaled League Co-Founder

Collier is a co-founder of Unrivaled, a new 3-on-3 women's basketball league launching in 2025. Along with Breanna Stewart, she's working to create more opportunities and higher pay for women's basketball players during the WNBA offseason.
