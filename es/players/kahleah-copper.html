üìñ Biography

Kahleah Copper is one of the WNBA's most dynamic two-way players, combining elite athleticism with lockdown defense. Her journey from unheralded draft pick to Finals MVP is one of the league's great development stories.

üèÜ 2021 Finals MVP: Copper exploded in the 2021 WNBA Finals, leading the Chicago Sky to their first championship with dominant performances on both ends. She averaged 17.0 PPG in the Finals while guarding the opponent's best player.

Born on February 20, 1995, Copper played college basketball at Rutgers, where she developed into an elite defender. Selected in the 2nd round of the 2016 draft by Washington, she was later traded to Chicago where she blossomed into a star.

After seven seasons with the Sky, Copper was traded to Phoenix in 2024, joining Diana Taurasi and Brittney Griner. She represented Team USA at the 2024 Paris Olympics, winning gold.

Known for her explosive first step, acrobatic finishes, and suffocating defense, Copper is one of the most well-rounded wings in the WNBA.
