Han Xu is a towering Chinese center who has made history as one of the tallest and most promising Asian players in WNBA history. At 6'10" (208 cm), she combines rare size with developing skill, representing the next generation of Chinese basketball talent.

Born on October 31, 1999, in Shijiazhuang, Hebei Province, Han Xu's exceptional height made her a natural fit for basketball. She developed through China's youth basketball system, quickly establishing herself as one of the country's top prospects. Her performances in the WCBA and with China's national team caught the attention of WNBA scouts.

Selected 14th overall by the New York Liberty in the 2019 WNBA Draft, Han became the second Chinese player ever drafted into the WNBA. After splitting time between the WNBA and WCBA in her early career, she became a more regular contributor to the Liberty, using her size to impact games on both ends of the floor. In 2024, she won a WNBA championship with New York, becoming the first Chinese player to win a WNBA title.

Han continues to develop her game, working on her mobility, shooting range, and post skills. A key member of China's national team, she has competed in FIBA World Cups and Asian championships, helping to maintain China's presence in international basketball. As she gains experience and confidence, Han Xu represents hope for the future of Chinese basketball and the global expansion of the women's game.
