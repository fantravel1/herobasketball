üìñ Biography

Sheryl Swoopes made history as the first player ever signed to the WNBA, becoming the face of a new era in women's professional basketball. Known as the "female Michael Jordan," she was the first player to have a Nike shoe named after her.

üåü "Sheryl Swoopes didn't just join the WNBA. She WAS the WNBA."

Born on March 25, 1971, in Brownfield, Texas, Swoopes led Texas Tech to the NCAA Championship in 1993, scoring a record 47 points in the title game. She was the cornerstone of the Houston Comets dynasty that won the first four WNBA championships (1997-2000).

A three-time WNBA MVP (2000, 2002, 2005) and three-time Defensive Player of the Year (2000, 2002, 2003), Swoopes revolutionized the wing position with her two-way dominance. She was inducted into the Naismith Basketball Hall of Fame in 2016.

Beyond her on-court achievements, Swoopes broke barriers by becoming the first active professional player in a major American team sport to publicly come out as gay, paving the way for future generations of LGBTQ+ athletes.
