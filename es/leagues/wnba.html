üìñ About the WNBA

The Women's National Basketball Association (WNBA) is the premier professional women's basketball league in the world. Founded in 1996 as a counterpart to the NBA, the league began play in 1997 with eight teams.

The WNBA has grown to 12 teams and continues to expand. The league has produced legendary players like Lisa Leslie, Diana Taurasi, Sue Bird, and current stars A'ja Wilson and Caitlin Clark. The 2024 season saw unprecedented growth in viewership and attendance.
