üìñ Team History

The Dallas Wings relocated from Tulsa in 2016, bringing WNBA basketball to the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex. While still seeking their first championship, the franchise has assembled one of the league's most exciting young cores.

Built around dynamic scorer Arike Ogunbowale and versatile forward Satou Sabally, the Wings feature two of the most talented young players in the WNBA. Both were top-5 draft picks and represent the future of the franchise.

The team plays at College Park Center on the campus of UT Arlington, with plans for a new arena in the works. Dallas sports fans are increasingly embracing the Wings as the team builds toward contention.

üî• Arike Ogunbowale

The 2019 5th overall pick has become one of the WNBA's premier scorers. Known for her clutch gene (remember her NCAA Championship-winning shots for Notre Dame?), Arike has led Dallas in scoring every season since joining the team. She's a multiple-time All-Star and won the 2021 WNBA All-Star Game MVP.

üèÜ Franchise Legacy: Tulsa/Detroit

The franchise dates back to the Detroit Shock, who won three WNBA Championships (2003, 2006, 2008). After relocating to Tulsa (2010-2015) and then Dallas, the current Wings aim to restore championship glory to the franchise.
