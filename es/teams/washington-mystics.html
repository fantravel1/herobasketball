üìñ Team History

The Washington Mystics are one of the WNBA's original expansion teams, founded in 1998. After years of building, the franchise captured its first championship in 2019 behind the stellar play of MVP Elena Delle Donne.

The 2019 championship team swept the Connecticut Sun 3-0 in the Finals, with Delle Donne playing through a back injury and Finals MVP Emma Meesseman delivering clutch performances. The team also featured Kristi Toliver, Natasha Cloud, and Ariel Atkins.

Recent seasons have been challenging due to injuries to Delle Donne, but the franchise remains committed to building around their star when healthy. The team plays at the Entertainment & Sports Arena in southeast Washington.

Two-time MVP (2015, 2019), Elena Delle Donne is one of the most efficient scorers in WNBA history. Her 50-40-90 shooting season in 2019 remains one of the greatest individual campaigns ever, leading Washington to its first title.
