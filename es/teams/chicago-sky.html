üìñ Team History

The Chicago Sky joined the WNBA as an expansion team in 2006 and won their first-ever championship in 2021. The team has become one of the league's most exciting franchises, known for entertaining basketball and star power.

The 2021 championship team featured Candace Parker returning to her hometown, Courtney Vandersloot, Allie Quigley, and Finals MVP Kahleah Copper. They defeated the Phoenix Mercury in four games to bring Chicago its first WNBA title.

After that championship core departed, the Sky entered a rebuild in 2024, drafting Angel Reese and Kamilla Cardoso to build a young, physical frontcourt. Despite a losing record, Reese's record-breaking rookie season brought national attention to the franchise.
