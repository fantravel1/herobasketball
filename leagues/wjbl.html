2020 Tokyo Olympics - Silver Medal

Japan made history at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, winning the silver medal - their best-ever Olympic finish in women's basketball. Led by point guard Rui Machida and coached by Tom Hovasse, Japan defeated France, Belgium, and other powerhouses with their signature fast-paced, three-point heavy attack. This historic run has energized women's basketball in Japan.

What is the WJBL?

The WJBL (Women's Japan Basketball League), also known as the W League, is Japan's premier women's basketball league, founded in 1967. It features 14 teams and is known for developing the fast-paced, team-oriented style that led Japan to a historic silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics. Teams are backed by major Japanese corporations.

Has Japan had success in international women's basketball?

Yes, Japan won the silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, their best-ever finish. They have also won multiple Asian Games gold medals and 5 FIBA Asia Cup titles. The national team's success is built on players developed through the WJBL system and their unique style of play.

What is Japan's playing style?

Japanese women's basketball is known for its speed, precision passing, and exceptional three-point shooting. Despite generally shorter rosters, Japan emphasizes ball movement, team chemistry, and conditioning to compete against larger opponents. They often lead the world in three-point percentage.
